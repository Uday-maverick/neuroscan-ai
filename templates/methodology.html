Methodology

Research Approach

This system employs deep learning techniques to analyze structural brain MRI images and classify them into categories representing different stages of cognitive impairment.

The classification process involves preprocessing MRI images, extracting relevant features, and generating predictions with associated confidence scores and attention visualizations.

Classification Process

When an MRI image is uploaded, the system:

  • Preprocesses the image to standardize format and resolution
  • Analyzes the image using trained deep learning models
  • Generates classification predictions with confidence scores
  • Produces attention visualizations showing model focus areas

Explainability

The system provides Grad-CAM++ visualizations to help researchers understand which regions of the input image the model attends to when making classification decisions. These visualizations are research tools and should not be interpreted as clinical diagnostic indicators.

For detailed information about the explainability approach, see the Explainability page.

Research Limitations: This system was developed and validated under controlled research conditions. Generalization to other datasets, imaging protocols, or clinical settings has not been established. The system should be considered a research prototype and not a validated clinical tool.
